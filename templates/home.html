What is Skin Cancer?

Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, often caused by prolonged exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation. It can develop on areas of the skin exposed to the sun, but it may also occur in areas not ordinarily exposed to sunlight.

There are three main types of skin cancer:

  • Basal cell carcinoma: Often occurs on sun-exposed areas and rarely spreads.
  • Squamous cell carcinoma: Can spread more easily than basal cell carcinoma but is treatable if detected early.
  • Melanoma: The most dangerous form of skin cancer, known for its ability to spread to other parts of the body.

Skin cancer is preventable and treatable if caught early. Awareness and timely action are the keys to combating this condition.
